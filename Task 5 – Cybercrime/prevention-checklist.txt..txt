================================================================
     STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION TIPS
     Cybercrime Awareness 2025 | College Students, India
================================================================

1.  KEEP SOFTWARE & OS UPDATED
    Always install security patches as soon as they are released.
    Most ransomware attacks exploit vulnerabilities that already
    have fixes available. Enable automatic updates on all devices.

2.  USE STRONG & UNIQUE PASSWORDS
    Never reuse the same password across multiple accounts. Use at
    least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and
    symbols. A password manager like Bitwarden can help you manage
    them safely.

3.  ENABLE TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA)
    Add 2FA to your email, social media, banking apps, and college
    portal. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks
    unauthorised access. Prefer an authenticator app over
    SMS-based 2FA.

4.  THINK BEFORE YOU CLICK — SPOT PHISHING
    Do not click links or open attachments from unknown senders.
    Verify the sender's actual email address carefully. When in
    doubt, visit the website directly by typing the URL yourself
    in the browser.

5.  BACK UP YOUR FILES REGULARLY
    Keep copies of academic work and personal documents on an
    external drive or trusted cloud storage. Follow the 3-2-1
    rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite. Backups are
    your only real defence against ransomware.

6.  AVOID PUBLIC WI-FI FOR SENSITIVE TASKS
    Public Wi-Fi in cafes, libraries, and hostels can be monitored
    by attackers. Never access banking apps or enter passwords on
    unsecured networks. Use a VPN if you must connect to public
    Wi-Fi.

7.  DOWNLOAD APPS ONLY FROM OFFICIAL SOURCES
    Install apps only from the Google Play Store, Apple App Store,
    or the official website of the software. Pirated software and
    cracked apps are a leading source of malware infections on
    student devices.

8.  REVIEW APP PERMISSIONS REGULARLY
    Check what permissions your installed apps are requesting. A
    flashlight or calculator app does not need access to your
    contacts, camera, or location. Revoke unnecessary permissions
    in your phone settings.

9.  [UPI/FINANCIAL] NEVER SHARE YOUR UPI PIN OR OTP
    Legitimate banks and government agencies will NEVER ask for
    your PIN or OTP over a call or message. Fraudsters create
    panic — stay calm and hang up. Remember: you only enter your
    UPI PIN to SEND money, never to receive it.

10. [UPI/FINANCIAL] VERIFY QR CODES & PAYMENT REQUESTS
    Scammers send fake collect requests on UPI that actually
    deduct money from your account. Always check the recipient
    name shown on your app before confirming. Never accept payment
    requests from unknown people.

11. LOG OUT FROM SHARED DEVICES
    Always log out of your accounts when using college lab
    computers, library systems, or a friend's device. Do not save
    passwords on shared devices. Clear the browser history
    after use.

12. BE CAREFUL ON SOCIAL MEDIA
    Avoid sharing personal details like your phone number, home
    address, college ID, or daily routine publicly. Cybercriminals
    use social media to build profiles for targeted phishing and
    identity theft attacks.
